## 1. Heidi's 15-million-visit UK report — counterpoint to yesterday's *npj* critique

**Heidi Health** has published the largest real-world ambient-scribe dataset in the NHS to date — **15 million UK patient interactions** across ten independent service evaluations through October 2025. Headlines: **86% reduction in documentation time**, **4 million clinician hours returned**, **95% reporting burnout improvement**. The detail is more useful. At **Modality Partnership**, in-consultation documentation roughly halved and **after-hours paperwork fell 61%**. An anonymised ED cut average discharge-letter processing from **9.03 days to 2.6 minutes**; a separate clinic cleared a 2,700-letter backlog in four months. Heidi now reports it handles **over 500,000 UK patient interactions per week**.

**The view from the surgery:** Pair this with yesterday's *npj Digital Medicine* paper on where scribes break and the picture sharpens: the GP-room win is now properly documented; the ICU and A&E gap is still real. The vendor-funded glow is unmistakable, but the underlying evaluations are NHS-led and the sample size makes selection bias hard to argue. If your PCN is still deciding, this is the dataset to actually read.

## 2. Brussels' *Digital Omnibus on AI* — healthcare gets 16 more months, or 16 more months of fudge

The European Commission's **Digital Omnibus on AI**, agreed on **7 May**, grants **high-risk AI in healthcare, hiring and education a 16-month extension** on the demanding compliance obligations originally due in **August 2026**. Conformity assessment and post-market surveillance now slide toward **December 2027**. Prohibited practices remain enforceable. For AI already regulated as a medical device, the August 2027 transition window is unchanged.

**Why it matters:** Read this honestly. The medtech lobby pitched the extension as relief from unworkable timelines; the quieter Commission-internal pitch was that **no member state had stood up the AI Act Service Desks** needed to actually do conformity assessments at scale. The MHRA has been keeping its own counsel on whether to mirror, diverge or wait — and the answer matters, because a large share of AI-scribe suppliers operating in NHS primary care are EU-incorporated.

## 3. AMA calls doctor deepfakes a public-health crisis — the UK has no parallel framework

The **American Medical Association**, off the back of an investigation surfacing **over 100 AI-generated deepfake doctor videos** on TikTok and Instagram, this week called on Congress to **legislate against synthetic-media impersonation of physicians**. AMA CEO **John Whyte** named it a public-health crisis. Real clinicians — including dermatologist **Dr Joel Bervell** — have been cloned to flog scam Ozempic-alternatives. A separate **Radiology** paper this spring found **most clinicians could not reliably spot deepfake X-rays**, which moves the threat surface from marketing fraud into clinical pathways.

**The view:** The GMC currently treats this under standard "uphold public trust" language. There is no statutory tort of physician impersonation in England and Wales, and the Online Safety Act's takedown regime is slow and patchwork. Worth checking your own image hasn't been lifted from a practice website — most colleagues find out when a patient asks about a product they have "endorsed".

## 4. Anthropic buys Stainless for $300m+ — the SDK plumbing under OpenAI and Google

**Anthropic** confirmed on **18 May** the acquisition of **Stainless**, the four-year-old developer-tools company founded by ex-Stripe engineer **Alex Rattray**, for a reported **$300m+**. Stainless quietly builds the **Python, TypeScript, Go, Java, Kotlin and Ruby SDKs** that **OpenAI, Google and Meta** ship to their own developers — the libraries every external app uses to call their models. Going forward, those tools sit inside an Anthropic subsidiary.

**Why it matters:** This is an infrastructure story, not a model story — the kind of move that does not show up in benchmark charts. Owning the SDK toolchain that produces your rivals' developer libraries is a slow, structural advantage. For the NHS the relevant version is whether **agentic clinical AI** procured in 2027 sits behind libraries one of the two AI duopolists has effective title-deed over.

## 5. MHRA AI Commission goes public tomorrow — the shape of UK regulation, three months early

The **National Commission into the Regulation of AI in Healthcare**, chaired by **Professor Alastair Denniston** with MHRA chief executive **Lawrence Tallon**, hosts its **public webinar tomorrow, Wednesday 20 May**. The Commission's substantive recommendations are due in summer — on track for a **July white paper**. Over **770 written responses** were lodged in the call for evidence that closed in February. The recurring themes flagged in the Commission's own summary are **liability allocation, post-market surveillance, and where consumer-grade chatbots sit relative to SaMD**.

**The view from the surgery:** Worth an hour. The clinical-AI policy ecosystem has been waiting on this group's framing for six months. Denniston chaired the NHS AI Lab evaluation framework that the FDA quietly adopted in 2023; his thinking on multi-loop human oversight is more developed than the Brussels equivalent.

### From the Eye

Three the front pages didn't run. **Item one** — *The AI Daily Brief* this week flagged enterprise survey data showing **Anthropic's Claude has overtaken ChatGPT inside US businesses for the first time** — **34.4% of paying enterprises** on Claude versus **32.3% on ChatGPT**. The plot point is not the swap; it is that **enterprise adoption flipped without a single new model release** — a function of pricing, integrations and contracts, which is what the post-honeymoon AI market actually looks like. **Item two** — **Azeem Azhar's** 10 May *Exponential View* takes seriously Anthropic researcher **Jack Clark's** stated **60% probability that a frontier model trains its own successor by 2028** — the recursive-intelligence-explosion case Azhar has previously been politely sceptical of. Worth a read for the way he flips through the hiring patterns and capex commitments that would have to be visible *now* if any of the major labs actually believed it. **Item three** — **GPonline** carried the story of a patient in the Midlands who **turned up at his GP practice for an appointment that did not exist**, having been confidently told the time and date by **Meta AI inside WhatsApp**. The practice has no integration with Meta. There is no investigation pathway for this kind of error, no MHRA jurisdiction, no GMC route. Quietly it is the cleanest example yet of consumer-chatbot harm that lands directly in the GP appointment book.
