## Fable 5 Raises the Bar for AI Ambition

_Anthropic ships Fable 5 — the first Mythos-class model and, fairly undisputedly, the best AI we've ever used. But at this level, getting the most out of state-of-the-art isn't your old prompts with a new model: it's controversial guardrails, usage-based reality, and a new skill called task imagination._

## The One Idea

### Fable 5 doesn't just beat the benchmarks — it raises the bar for ambition.

Anthropic's first Mythos-class model is a genuine leap, especially on agentic coding, where it one-shots things that used to take teams months. But the real story is what it demands of us: in a usage-based world, we have to become token-efficiency optimizers who match models to use cases, and we have to develop "task imagination" — the ability to hand an agent days of responsibility, not minutes of tasks. The frontier has moved from answers to tasks to responsibilities, and most of us aren't dreaming big enough to use it.

## The Other Side

Against NLW's claim that Fable 5 'raises the bar' and proves the frontier is still leaping, Zitron argues the opposite: that progress is slowing and each release delivers less. He calls the booster excitement 'a con centered around a technology to ingratiate at the highest cost possible,' and says benchmark theatrics distract from the only number that matters, the roughly two trillion dollars in annual revenue the build-out must generate by 2030 to justify itself. A model that one-shots an impressive demo, in his telling, does nothing to close that gap.

Source: Ed Zitron, "AI Is Slowing Down", 8 June 2026 — https://www.wheresyoured.at/ai-is-slowing-down/

## The Truth Beneath

The single variable both men orbit is the rate of real, economically useful capability gain. Both accept the benchmarks jumped and that the model produces striking demos. The empirical question is whether Fable-class capability converts into durable revenue at the scale the infrastructure requires, and the observable that settles it is compute-cost-adjusted enterprise revenue growth measured against the roughly two-trillion-dollar-by-2030 bogey. The residue is worldview: NLW sees one-shotting and hundred-hour runs as a step change in what is possible; Zitron sees demonstrations that do not pay the interest on the data centres. NLW is vindicated if agentic-coding revenue compounds fast enough to service the build-out; Zitron is vindicated if revenue growth flattens while capex balloons. Both are polemicists with incentives, NLW bullish and anti-doom while selling transformation, Zitron bubble-certain and anti-hype while selling the autopsy, so the signal is that both treat the gap between capability and required revenue as the real test. Watch hyperscaler AI revenue run-rate against capex over the next two quarters.

## GP's Corner

_Claude's notes on what today means for UK general practice and the NHS - commentary, not from the podcast itself._

### Over-tuned safety filters are a clinical problem

Fable 5 reportedly refused basic biology, flagging 'cancer' as a biosecurity risk and pausing on 'mitochondria'. For a GP or Medical Examiner using a general AI to sketch a differential or explain a mechanism, an over-zealous classifier can block a legitimate clinical query. If you hit a refusal, that is the filter misfiring, not the medicine; rephrase or use a properly clinical tool.

### Task imagination, GP edition

NLW's 'hand it responsibilities, not tasks' idea has a safe, modest NHS version: not 'diagnose this patient' but 'watch my inbox of normal results and draft the routine recall letters for me to check'. The win is delegating bounded, auditable admin with a clinician always signing off, which is exactly the scribe model that already works.

### If the leap is real, if it isn't

If NLW is right that this is a genuine step change, the practical question for practices is governance: who checks the agent's work. If Zitron is right that it is oversold, the cost of waiting is low and early-adopter trusts can find the failure modes first. Either way, adopt only where the output is checkable by a clinician.

## NLW's Takes

### The first model that disagrees — and updates without kowtowing

NLW's standout everyday win: in a strategic debate, Fable disagreed clearly, then updated its position on new information without collapsing into telling him what he wanted to hear. That alone is a massive upgrade for strategic ideation, a top real-world use case.

### NLW: Fable rebuilt three of his own products in single shots

In hours of unattended work, Fable rebuilt Superintelligent's audit input system on Whisper, the Agent Transformation Intensive site and platform, and turned AIDB's shareable-nuggets mockups into a real production pipeline. The takeaway: far less management, much bigger ambition.

### The new skill: task imagination

With models that can run for days, NLW argues we'll all have to up-level ambition — and become token-efficiency optimizers who match models to use cases. Nate B. Jones's framing: most of us have nothing that's ever taken even an hour on AI, so the scarce skill is imagining tasks worth handing to a model that works for days.
