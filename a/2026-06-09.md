# David's AI Daily Brief

**Tuesday 09 June 2026 · 30 min listen**

## OpenAI Declares the Next Phase of AI

_OpenAI files to go public and declares its third phase on the same day — while Apple's fine-but-minimal Siri reboot sharpens a bigger question: are consumer AI and work AI still the same technology?_

Source: https://aidailybrief.ai/e/2026-06-09

Audio: https://davids-ai-brief.netlify.app/audio/2026-06-09.mp3

## The One Idea

### The fork is here: consumer AI and work AI may be two different things.

OpenAI declared its third phase the same day it filed to go public. Apple shipped a Siri that's finally fine. Put them side by side and the conclusion gets hard to avoid: the thing we lump together as "AI" — the chatbot that answers questions and the fleets of synthetic employees reshaping work — might be splitting into separate technologies. And maybe it's time we started talking about them that way.

## The Other Side

Zitron's answer to OpenAI declaring a grand new 'phase' is that the underlying business still shows no demonstrable return. He argues nobody can actually measure AI's return on investment, or even the cost of a single task, because token-based billing makes spend unpredictable and hallucination makes output unreliable. He points to Uber reportedly burning its annual token budget in four months and a company that 'accidentally spent $500 million in the space of a month' on models. Splitting AI into consumer and work tiers, on his reading, is market segmentation, not proof that either one pays for itself.

Source: Ed Zitron, "AI Doesn't Have ROI", 2 June 2026 — https://www.wheresyoured.at/ai-doesnt-have-roi/

## The Truth Beneath

The single variable both men orbit is whether work AI produces measurable economic value that exceeds its cost. Both accept enterprise spend is exploding and that billing has moved to tokens. The empirical question that will resolve it is whether firms keep paying once they can actually measure cost against benefit, and the observable that settles it is audited enterprise renewal rates and net AI margins, not vibes. What is left is pure worldview: NLW reads exploding usage as proof of transformative value; Zitron reads it as unmeasured, possibly destructive spend. NLW is vindicated if heavy users renew and report hard productivity gains at current prices; Zitron is vindicated if spend collapses or stays unjustifiable once measured. Both are polemicists with incentives, NLW bullish and anti-doom while selling adoption, Zitron bubble-certain and anti-hype while selling the takedown, so the signal is that both now concede cost measurement is the central problem. Watch the first clean enterprise ROI data after token billing fully lands.

## GP's Corner

_Claude's notes on what today means for UK general practice and the NHS - commentary, not from the podcast itself._

### Two different tools for two different jobs

The consumer-versus-work split maps neatly onto general practice: a chatbot drafting a patient leaflet is a very different beast from an agent reconciling a discharge summary against the problem list. If NLW is right that work AI is the real engine, the NHS value sits in unglamorous back-office automation, not the patient-facing chatbot. If Zitron is right that none of it has measurable ROI yet, adopt only where you can audit the time saved, as you can with a scribe that lets you count minutes per consultation.

### Token billing is coming to your software

Both men flag the shift from flat subscriptions to pay-per-use, and it will reach NHS procurement. A scribe priced per consultation behaves very differently from a flat licence when winter pressures double your throughput. Ask any vendor now how costs scale with use before signing.

### What patients will bring through the door

As OpenAI's consumer ambitions get louder, expect more patients arriving with chatbot-generated interpretations of their symptoms. A 'consumer-fine' Siri that finally does the basics is a reminder these tools are polished but not clinical; gentle signposting to NHS resources still matters.

## NLW's Takes

### This transition may matter more than ChatGPT's launch did

The agentic shift of November-to-January and the subsidy-to-shortage shift of the last month are one mega-transition — the second great transition of generative AI. For the ultimate shape of AI in society, the agent era and its economics are likely the more significant one.

### ChatGPT might be becoming a distraction

Seat-based ChatGPT revenue looks "ridiculously inconsequential" next to Codex API usage. OpenAI will never kill it — it's the top of the funnel and the great public-markets differentiator against Anthropic — but watch where the energy actually goes.

### What would change if we let consumer AI matter less?

Less AI shoved into every app? Less public anger at an all-encompassing meme? Consumer AI hasn't failed — chatbots are deeply embedded in daily life — but there's no comparison to the tonnage of impact coming from agentic work AI. Maybe it's time to talk about them as separate things.
